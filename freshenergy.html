Fresh Energy
iOS & Android & Web, UI Design & UX Design & Communication Design

Making sense of electricity consumption and opening opportunities in a new, growing market.

Fresh Energy builds customer-centric products based on smart meter data, with a focus on helping users understand their energy consumption. Based in Berlin, they’ve raised over €6m since 2017.



The challenge

Taking over an existing project and reworking the UX and UI without alienating existing users — while adding features aligned with business goals. Also, product had to be designed in a way that allowed whitelabeling, since that was part of the business model. I was requested to come up with a new brand solution as well.

My process

Initial interviews with all stakeholders aligned me on business and product goals. To identify user goals and define a feature roadmap, we surveyed our users via Facebook groups and email — and invited dozens for in-house interviews. I facilitated design sprints to bring new features to life. A deep dive into finding company values through multiple sessions with the management set the tables for a complete brand redesign.


Solutions

I started by decreasing the Customer Aquisition Cost and redesigning the information architecture of all apps — having in mind the whitelabeling requirements — while ideating features aligned with business requirements. Following a rebrand, a new visual style was applied.

CAC was reduced by -20% and 4 new whitelabel partners signed up. 3/10 most requested features (by public vote) were rolled out.


Information Architecture

Tab bar navigation replaced the old side-menu navigation. Based on user interviews, new sections such as profile and household information were added to their expected places. To support future business goals, a services section was added as well.


Main use cases — checking live and historic electricity consumption — were simplified and enriched with new ways of displaying data

Reducing cost to serve

To reduce cost to serve, edit functionality was added for personal information. Weekly average was 6 support calls per 1000 users.

Meeting business and user goals

By leveraging the data we had and working with product and business teams, I was able to define innovative services. By basing them on existing data, we saved precious development time and reduced time to market.

Before building a service, we wanted to assess its viability with a simple pilot project. Pilot projects were done through email communication and manual work. After confirming the demand, we went ahead with the actual implementation in the product. Design was tested during and after the UI/UX phase.

Refresh — service 1

“Refresh” works with existing data. With insight into our users dishwasher usage we can send them dishwasher tabs just before they run out.

Fridge recommendation — service 2

Users fridge consumption is compared to our database. Based on that we show our users potential savings — together with the amortization time.
