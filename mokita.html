Mokita — Web
UI Design, UX Design, Communication Design


Breaking information asymmetry in the Forex market.

Mokita or "the truth we all know but agree not to talk about" is a financial tool predicting the best time for currency conversion. By analyzing vast amount of data, Mokita exploits information asymmetry to level out the playing field between providers and end-users.


The challenge

Instill trust in Mokita users — which is always challenging in a financial context. Communicate clearly should the user transfer their funds now — and which transfer provider they should use.

My process

Initially, detailed interviews with stakeholders were carried out to understand their competition, vision, and goals for the product and the brand — near and long term. The MVP was designed and tested via inVision — with revisions after user testing.


Results

Mokita predicts a good transfer window based on macro FX trends. Users can easily find out if now is a good time to transfer their money — or get notified on time. The best FX providers are easily searchable for each currency pair.


Communicating clearly

Mokita analyzes past FX rates from a multitude of providers and gives a simple YES/NO feedback regarding the current rate.

Users can save their favourite currency pairs for quick access — a nd get reminded when the rates are cheap.

Transfer providers are listed with cost and transfer-time easily visible. This helps users decide on the best transfer provider for their currency pair.

Design is fully responsive to provide with a consistent experience on all platforms.

Brand design

A detailed questionnaire was used to recognize how the clients saw the Mokita brand. This included questions such as "If Mokita was a car brand, which one it would be?", and "Is Mokita accessible or exclusive?".

Blue color was used to instill trust, and an arrow was used as a building element — communicating direct and fast transfers.

I designed detailed brand guidelines as well.
